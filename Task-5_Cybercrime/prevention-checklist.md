# Stay Safe Online Checklist: 

1. Use different passwords for different accounts—even though it’s tempting to reuse one. It really makes a difference.

2. Turn on two-factor authentication wherever possible, especially for email, banking, and social media. It adds an extra layer of safety.

3. Be careful about what you share online. Personal details like your phone number, address, or college info shouldn’t be public.

4. If a link or message looks suspicious or too urgent (“click now or your account will be blocked”), pause and double-check before clicking.

5. Try not to log into important accounts while using public Wi-Fi in cafes or college spaces. It’s not always secure.

6. Remember: you never need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. If someone asks you to do that, it’s a scam.

7. Avoid scanning random QR codes or accepting unknown payment requests, even if someone claims they’re sending you money.

8. Make it a habit to quickly check your bank or UPI transactions once in a while—you can catch problems early.

9. Keep your apps and phone updated. Those updates often fix security issues you might not even know about.

10. If something feels off or confusing, don’t rush. Take a moment or ask someone before acting.

11. Report it immediately on the National Cyber Crime Portal: cybercrime.gov.in

12. Call the Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 as soon as possible
