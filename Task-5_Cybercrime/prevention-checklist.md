# Stay Safe Online Checklist: 

1. I think it is an idea to use different passwords for different accounts even though it can be tempting to reuse one password. This really makes a difference when it comes to keeping your accounts safe.

2. You should turn on two-factor authentication wherever it is possible for things like email, banking and social media accounts. This adds a layer of safety to protect your accounts.

3. Be careful about what you share because Personal details like your phone number, address or college information should not be public for everyone to see.

4. If a link or message looks suspicious or seems urgent like it says "click now or your account will be blocked" then you should pause and double-check before you click on it.

5. Try not to log into your accounts when you are using public Wi-Fi in places, like cafes or college spaces because it is not always secure.

6. Remember you never need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money so if someone asks you to do that it is probably a scam.

7. You should avoid scanning QR codes or accepting unknown payment requests even if someone claims they are sending you money because it could be a trick.

8. Make it a habit to quickly check your bank or UPI transactions every now and then so you can catch any problems on.

9. Keep your apps and phone updated, because those updates often fix security issues that one might not even know about and this will help keep your accounts safe.

10. If something feels off or confusing do not rush into anything take a moment to think or ask someone you trust for help before acting.

11. If you think you have been a victim of cyber crime you should report it immediately on the National Cyber Crime Portal, which is- cybercrime.gov.in.

12. You can also call the Cyber Crime Helpline at 1930 as soon as possible and they will be able to help you with your problem.
